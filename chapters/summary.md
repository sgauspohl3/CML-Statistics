# Course Summary

The big ideas, stitched back together:

- **Descriptive statistics** describes what we see — center, spread, shape, and plots.
- **Probability** gives us the language for uncertainty — distributions, random variables, Bayes' theorem.
- **Inferential statistics** lets us reason from samples to populations — fitting, estimating, testing.
- **Responsible practice** means quantifying uncertainty, documenting assumptions, and staying alert to the unknown knowns.

The most useful skill isn't memorizing formulas. It's knowing **which tool to reach for, and what its assumptions are** — and being honest about what you don't know.

## A few principles worth keeping

```{epigraph}
Always pair point estimates with uncertainty. A mean without a CI is half a sentence.

-- A working statistician's habit
```

```{epigraph}
Visualize before you summarize. A histogram or ECDF takes thirty seconds and saves you from believing the wrong thing.

-- Also good practice
```

```{epigraph}
Statistical significance is not practical significance. p < 0.05 is the beginning of the conversation, not the end.

-- Modern statistical guidance
```

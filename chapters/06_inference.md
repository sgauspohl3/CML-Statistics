# Inferential Statistics

Descriptive statistics tells you what's in your data. **Inferential statistics** tells you what your data implies about the world.

We can almost never measure an entire population — every voter, every product unit, every patient with a condition. Instead, we collect a *sample* and ask: what does this tell me about the *population* it came from?

This part covers:

- Sampling and the populations samples represent
- The two theorems that make inference work — the Law of Large Numbers and the Central Limit Theorem
- Methods for fitting distributions to data
- What makes an estimator good
- Confidence intervals (analytical and bootstrapped)
- Assessing goodness of fit
- Hypothesis testing
- ANOVA

# Descriptive Statistics

Before we can infer, we have to describe. Descriptive statistics is the toolkit for summarizing what's in front of us — center, spread, shape, and visualizations that let the data speak.

This part covers:

- Measures of **central tendency** (mean, median, mode)
- Measures of **variability** (variance, standard deviation, range, IQR)
- The **shape** of a distribution (skew, kurtosis)
- **Plotting** distributions (histograms, ECDFs)

Each of these is a lens. A skilled analyst rarely looks through just one.
